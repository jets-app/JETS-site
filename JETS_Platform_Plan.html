How the System Works (Step by Step)

1. Parent Applies

Parent creates account → Fills out 10-step form → Uploads photo → Adds 2 references → Pays $500 fee → Submits
  • Progress saves automatically — parent can leave and come back
  • Discount codes can reduce or waive the $500 fee
  • Parent enters referee's name, email, and phone number for each of the 2 recommendations
  • References automatically receive an email + SMS with a private link to fill out the recommendation form
  • Parents can see recommendation status (Pending / Submitted checkmark) but cannot see the content — content goes directly to admin only
  • Admin can track recommendation status and resend requests if needed
Application cannot be submitted until ALL of these are complete:
✓ All 10 form steps filled out (no empty required fields)
✓ Student photo uploaded
✓ Both recommendation letters received (submitted by referees)
✓ $500 application fee paid

The "Submit Application" button stays disabled until all 4 requirements are met. A progress tracker on the parent dashboard shows exactly what's done and what's still pending.

2. Office Reviews → Forwards to Principals

Application lands in dashboard → Office checks completeness → Adds notes → Forwards to Principals
  • Dashboard shows: total applications, how many pending, what each student still needs
  • Can print entire application as a PDF
  • Checklist per student: fee paid? Recommendations in? Photo uploaded?

3. Principals Review → Move to Interview

Principals review full application → Call recommendation references → Look into the student → Good fit? Click "Move to Interview"
  • Principals see all application details, recommendations, and notes
  • Can add their own notes and comments
  • If student looks like a good fit → one click to move to interview step
  • If not a fit → can reject with reason

4. Interview → Accept

System emails parents Calendly link → Parents schedule interview → Interview happens → Principals click "Accept Student" → Goes back to Office
  • Automatic email + SMS sent to parents with Calendly link to book interview
  • System tracks: interview scheduled, completed, or no-show
  • After interview, principals decide: accept or reject
  • Accepted → application goes back to office for next steps

5. Office Sends Enrollment Documents

Once accepted, the office sends out the document package:

DocumentSent ToWhat It Does
Medical FormParentsHealth info + insurance details — doubles as a card the student can take to the doctor
Student HandbookParents AND Student
(sent separately, each signs their own copy)		School rules & policies — both parent and student must acknowledge and sign
Tuition ContractParentsFinancial agreement for tuition payment terms
Enrollment AgreementParentsFormal acceptance and enrollment paperwork
  • Works like DocuSign — recipients click a link, see the document, fill fields, sign with finger or mouse
  • All templates are editable by admin before sending
  • System tracks which documents are sent, viewed, and signed
  • Student handbook goes out twice — one copy to parents, one copy to student, signed independently

6. Scholarship (If Parents Want It)

Parents request scholarship → Office sends scholarship app → Parents fill out & submit → Admin reviews & decides → Two more docs sent
  • If parents want to apply for a scholarship, office sends them the scholarship application
  • Parents fill out full financial details + upload tax returns
  • Admin reviews with affordability assessment tool
  • Once scholarship amount is decided:
    • Tuition contract is UPDATED with the new adjusted tuition amount
    • Pay It Forward scholarship contract is sent to the STUDENT to sign

7. Officially Enrolled

Student becomes officially enrolled when ALL signed documents are received back:
✓ Medical form (signed by parents)
✓ Student handbook (signed by parents)
✓ Student handbook (signed by student)
✓ Tuition contract (signed by parents)
✓ Enrollment agreement (signed by parents)
✓ Scholarship contract (signed by student) — if applicable

Once all documents are in, the student's status automatically changes to ENROLLED — they are officially a student in the system.

8. Tuition & Payments

  • Admin sets a custom tuition amount per student
  • Flexible payment plans: pay in full, semester, quarterly, or monthly
  • Admin creates and sends invoices
  • Parents pay by credit card through the portal
  • Automatic receipts sent via email
  • All payments sync to QuickBooks Online automatically

6. Scholarship Applications

Matches the current "Pay It Forward" application with all sections:

  • Family info, father's & mother's income/assets/employment details
  • Expenses: taxes, housing (rent or own), utilities, auto, household
  • Requested scholarship amount + reason
  • Tax return uploads + signature/certification
  • Admin reviews and approves/denies with amount

7. Tuition Affordability Assessment

Built-in tool that determines how much tuition a family can actually afford — no third-party cost.

How it works: Parents fill out a detailed financial form + upload tax returns. The system calculates a recommended family contribution automatically. Admin reviews and can adjust.

What parents fill out:

  • Income: salary, business income, investments, rental income, government assistance, all other sources
  • Assets: bank balances (checking + savings), investments, real estate equity, retirement accounts
  • Liabilities: mortgage balance, car loans, student loans, credit card debt, other debt
  • Monthly expenses: housing, utilities, food, medical, auto, childcare, other tuition obligations
  • Household: family size, number of children in tuition-charging schools
  • Tax returns: upload 1-2 years of 1040s, W-2s, business returns if self-employed

What the system calculates:

  • Total family income minus essential expenses = available income
  • Recommended family contribution (as a percentage of available income)
  • Adjustments for number of children, other tuition, community factors

What admin sees:

  • Side-by-side comparison: what parents reported vs. what tax returns show
  • System's recommended contribution amount
  • Ability to override or adjust the recommendation
  • Optional: enter a third-party FACTS assessment recommendation for cross-reference
  • Historical tracking across all families and years
Why not credit scores or bank account APIs? We researched Plaid, Experian, Truework, etc. Credit checks require special legal permission (FCRA) that schools don't have. Bank APIs cost $6K-18K/year. No private school uses these — they all use self-reported data + tax verification, which is what we're building. Same result, zero extra cost.

Build Order (10 Sprints)

SPRINT 1 Foundation

Set up the project, database, login system, and base design. Everything else is built on top of this.

  • Project setup with premium design system (burgundy brand color)
  • Database with all tables for students, applications, users, etc.
  • Login & registration pages for parents
  • Role-based access: parents see their portal, staff see the admin dashboard
SPRINT 2 Parent Application Portal

The heart of the system — parents can create an account and fill out the full 10-step application.

  • All 10 form steps (see Application Form section above)
  • Auto-save progress — come back anytime
  • Photo upload for student
  • $500 application fee with discount codes
  • Parent dashboard showing their application status
SPRINT 3 Recommendation System

Automated, confidential recommendation requests — references get an email + SMS, fill out a form, it goes directly to the school.

  • Parent adds 2 references: name, email, phone number, relationship (past teacher, principal, rabbi, etc.)
  • System automatically emails each reference a unique private link (+ SMS notification)
  • Reference fills out the structured recommendation form (no account needed)
  • Recommendation content goes directly to admin only — parents see status (Pending/Submitted) but NOT the content
  • Both recommendations must be completed before the parent can submit the application
  • Admin/office/principals can view all recommendations for any application
  • Status tracking per referee: Pending → Sent → Viewed → Completed
  • Admin can resend the request if a referee hasn't responded
SPRINT 4 Admin Dashboard

The office staff's home base — see everything at a glance and manage applications.

  • Stats: total applications, pending, accepted, missing items
  • Searchable, filterable list of all applications
  • Click into any application to see full details
  • Add internal notes and annotations
  • Checklist per student: what they still need
SPRINT 5 Review & Acceptance Workflow

The full pipeline from application received to student accepted.

  • Office reviews application, forwards to principals
  • Principals review, call references, research the student
  • If good fit → click "Move to Interview" → system auto-emails Calendly link to parents
  • Track interview status: Scheduled / Completed / No-show
  • After interview, principals click "Accept" or "Reject"
  • Accepted → goes back to office to send documents
  • Full timeline of every step and who did what
  • Email + SMS notifications at each stage
SPRINT 6 Documents & E-Signing

Send documents to parents AND students for electronic signing — works like DocuSign.

  • Medical form → sent to parents
  • Student handbook → sent separately to parents AND student (each signs their own copy)
  • Tuition contract → sent to parents
  • Enrollment agreement → sent to parents
  • Scholarship contract → sent to student (if scholarship awarded)
  • Admin can edit any template before sending
  • Tuition contract can be UPDATED after scholarship decision (adjusted amount)
  • All documents signed electronically, stored as PDFs
  • Once ALL documents are signed → student status automatically changes to ENROLLED
